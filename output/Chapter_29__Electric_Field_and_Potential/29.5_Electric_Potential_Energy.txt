**Key Concepts aur Formulas (with real-world connection):**

*   **Electric Potential Energy (U):** Imagine aap do magnets ko ek dusre ke paas ya door le jaate ho. Aapko force ke against work karna padta hai, right? Similar way mein, jab aap charges ko move karte ho, electric field work karta hai, aur woh work potential energy ke form mein store hota hai.

    *   Formula (do charges ke liye):
        `U = (q1 * q2) / (4 * pi * epsilon_0 * r)`
        jahan,
        *   `q1` aur `q2` charges hain,
        *   `r` unke beech ki distance hai,
        *   `epsilon_0`  permittivity of free space hai (constant).

    *   **Real-world application:** Capacitors! Capacitors electric potential energy store karte hain, jiska use electronic devices jaise ki smartphones aur computers mein hota hai.

*   **Change in Potential Energy (∆U):**  Agar aap ek charge ko point A se point B tak move karte ho, toh potential energy change hoti hai.

    *   Formula: `∆U = U_final - U_initial`

*   **Zero Potential Energy:** Hum generally potential energy ko infinite separation per zero maante hain (`U(∞) = 0`). Isse calculations aasaan ho jaati hain.

*   **System of Multiple Charges:** Agar system mein bahut saare charges hain, toh total potential energy har pair of charges ke potential energy ka sum hoti hai.

**Common Question Patterns aur Approach:**

1.  **Potential Energy Calculation:** Tumhe charges aur unke beech ki distances di jaayengi, aur tumhe system ki potential energy calculate karni hogi. Formula use karo aur carefully values substitute karo. Units ka dhyan rakhna!

2.  **Work Done Problems:** Tumhe ek charge ko move karne mein work done calculate karna hoga. `Work Done = - Change in Potential Energy`

3.  **Conceptual Questions:**  Woh pooch sakte hain ki potential energy kab positive hogi, kab negative, ya zero. Yaad rakho, same charges (positive-positive ya negative-negative) repel karte hain, isliye unko paas laane mein work karna padta hai, aur potential energy positive hoti hai. Opposite charges attract karte hain, isliye unko door le jaane mein work karna padta hai, aur potential energy negative hoti hai.

**Quick Revision Points:**

*   Potential energy ek *scalar* quantity hai (direction nahi hoti).
*   Potential energy system ki *configuration* per depend karti hai, location per nahi.
*   Agar electric force *positive* work karta hai, potential energy *decrease* hoti hai.
*   Agar electric force *negative* work karta hai, potential energy *increase* hoti hai.

**Example Question Solving Strategy**

**Example 29.3** se ek question solve krte hain.

*   **Question:** Three particles, each having a charge of 10 µC, are placed at the vertices of an equilateral triangle of side 10 cm. Find the work done by a person in pulling them apart to infinite separations.
*   **Solution:**

    1.  **Initial Potential Energy:** System ki initial potential energy calculate karo. Since teen charges hain, teen pairs honge. Har pair ke liye potential energy hogi: `U_pair = (q^2) / (4 * pi * epsilon_0 * r)`. Total initial potential energy hogi `3 * U_pair`.
    2.  **Final Potential Energy:** Infinite separation per potential energy zero hoti hai.
    3.  **Work Done:** `Work Done = - Change in Potential Energy = - (Final Potential Energy - Initial Potential Energy) = - (0 - Initial Potential Energy) = Initial Potential Energy`
