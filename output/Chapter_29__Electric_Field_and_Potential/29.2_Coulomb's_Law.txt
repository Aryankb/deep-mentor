**Key Concepts and Formulas**

*   **Coulomb's Law kya hai?** Do charges ke beech mein jo force lagta hai, usko Coulomb's Law batata hai.

*   **Formula:**
    ```
    F = k * |q1 * q2| / r^2
    ```
    yahan,
    *   `F` force hai (attractive ya repulsive)
    *   `q1` aur `q2` charges hain
    *   `r` dono charges ke beech ka distance hai
    *   `k` Coulomb's constant hai (approximately 9 x 10^9 N m²/C²)

*   **Vector Form:** Force ki direction bhi important hai.  Vector form mein:

    ```
    F = k * q1 * q2 * r_hat / r^2
    ```

    yahan, `r_hat` unit vector hai jo ek charge se dusre charge ki taraf point karta hai.

*   **Permittivity of Free Space (ε₀):**  `k = 1 / (4 * pi * ε₀)`.  ε₀ ki value hoti hai 8.854 x 10^-12 C²/N m². Yeh batata hai ki electric field vacuum mein kitna easily ban sakta hai.

**Real-World Applications**

*   **Electrostatic Force in everyday life:**
    *   Photocopiers aur laser printers mein toner ko drum par attract karne ke liye.
    *   Electrostatic painting mein, paint particles ko charge kiya jata hai aur woh object par attract hote hain.
    *   Static electricity (jaise ki kapde chipakna).
*   **Atomic Structure:** Atoms mein electrons aur nucleus ke beech mein electrostatic force hi unko bandh ke rakhta hai.

**Common Question Patterns**

1.  **Direct Calculation:**
    *   **Question:** Do charges diye gaye hain, unke beech ka distance bhi diya gaya hai. Force calculate karo.
    *   **Approach:** Seedha formula use karo aur values plug in karo. Units ka dhyan rakho!

2.  **Force Direction:**
    *   **Question:** Do charges hain, batao force attractive hoga ya repulsive.
    *   **Approach:** Agar charges same sign ke hain (positive-positive ya negative-negative), toh repulsive. Agar opposite sign ke hain (positive-negative), toh attractive.

3.  **Net Force:**
    *   **Question:** Ek charge par do ya teen charges force laga rahe hain. Net force calculate karo.
    *   **Approach:** Har charge ki wajah se force calculate karo (vector form mein!), phir unko vectorially add karo.

4.  **Equilibrium:**
    *   **Question:** Ek charge ko kahan rakhein ki uspar net force zero ho?
    *   **Approach:** Forces ko balance karo.  Agar do charges hain, toh equilibrium point unke beech mein hoga (agar dono same sign ke hain) ya unke bahar hoga (agar opposite sign ke hain).

**Quick Revision Points**

*   Coulomb's Law inverse square law hai (force distance ke square ke inversely proportional hota hai).
*   Force vector quantity hai.
*   Charges add hote hain (algebraically).
*   `k` ki value yaad rakho (approximately 9 x 10^9 N m²/C²).
*   Attractive force negative hota hai aur repulsive force positive hota hai (in general).

**Non-Important / Theoretical Topics (Just a Line)**

*   "Charge is conserved" ka matlab hai ki total charge kabhi change nahi hota.
*   "Frictional electricity" mein rubbing se charges transfer hote hain.
*   "Induction" mein ek charged object dusre object mein charge redistribute karta hai.
